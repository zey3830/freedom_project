History

- 1400s-1500s
- The stock market officially started around this time period (without stocks)
- First stock market generally linked back to Belgium
- Antwerp, generally accepted as the first stock market system
- It’s the commercial center of Belgium
- The first market didn’t deal with stocks, instead handles affairs of government, businesses, and individual debt
- First publicly traded company (1602)
- The East Indian Company (EIC)
- Having all riches on one ship & traveling between lands is too risky
- EIC’s plan is to split the riches between many ships
- EIC released shares around 1602 & each investors with shares will get some EIC profits
- The selling of stocks first started in a coffee shop
- In other words, the first stock market is a coffee shop
- London Stock Exchange
- Officially formed in 1801
- Can’t trade stocks till 1825 due to a ban
- Main market for Europe
- New York Stock Exchange (NYSE)
- Formed in 1817
- Most powerful stock exchange
- Main market for America & the world
- Philadelphia Stock Exchange
- The first stock exchange (not NYSE)
- Modern
- Right now almost all countries have their own stock market
- 1971
- NASDAQ
- Created by the National Association of Securities Dealers & Financial Industry Regulatory Authority
- Online stock market
